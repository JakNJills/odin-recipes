Baozi(包子) Stuffed Steamed Pork Buns

Ingredients Description Instructions Back to Home

Description

Baozi(包子) is an extremely popular and favorite of many around the world. Not to be confused with jiaozi(another famous and popular Chinese Bao), the Baozi is a stuffed steamed bun. These buns are soft, fluffy, and stuffed with either meat, vegetables, sweetened red bean paste, or soup as the filling. This recipe will focus on helping you make stuffed pork steamed buns which are probably the most commonly made version. There is a legend behind the creation of Baozi. It is told that Zhang Liang created Baozi as a tribute to give to the gods or he used them to bribe a River God for his soldiers safety. Whether that is true or not scholars do believe they were created during the Han Dynasty in North China in the Xinjiang Province. Over time and distance the recipe has changed in several different ways.Warning if you continue with this recipe you will be making buns from scratch with this recipe. So you have been notified. Now get your mixers ready!

Ingredients

  • Dough
    • 5 grams(1 1/2 Tsp) Active Dry Yeast
    • 8 grams(2 Tsp) Granulated Sugar
    • 1 1/2 cups(355 milligrams) lukewarm Water
    • 580 grams(5-6 Cups) All Purpose Flour
      • Best to use Dumpling Flour from loval Chinese Market
  • Filling
    • 700 grams Pork
    • 2 Tsp Sesame Oil
    • 3 Tbsp Water
    • 1 1/2 Tsp Cornstarch
    • 2 Tbsp Ground Bean Sauce
    • 1 Tbsp Oyster Sauce
    • 1/2 Tsp White Pepper
    • 1 Tbsp Sweet Bean Sauce
    • 1 Tbsp Dark Soy Sauce
    • 3 Tbsp Vegetable Oil
    • 1 Tsp Sugar
    • 2 Tbsp Minced Ginger
    • 1 large Onion
    • 3 Finely Chopped Scallions
    • 2 Tbsp Shaoxing Wine
      • Can substitute another Chinese Rice Wine or dry cooking sherry
  • Tools
    • Parchment Paper
    • Plastic Wrap
    • Steamer

Instructions

  1. Making the Dough
    1. Pour the yeast and sugar in lukewarm water and let it set for 15 minutes. Once it foams at the top the yeast is actived and ready for use.
    2. Add Flour to a mixing bowl and slowly add the yeast mixture while mixing until a firm dough begins to form.
    3. Kneed the dough for 10 minutes until it smooth on the surface.
    4. Add dough ball into a big bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Store in a warm place for 1 hour or until the dough doubles in size.
  2. Making the Filling
    1. Cut and chop up your pork until it is ground
    2. Add the oil, the garlic, shallots, ginger, and scallions to a wok and cook until shallots are translucent.
    3. Remove aromatics, cook ground pork, and once the meat starts to change color add aromatics back into the wok and mix.
    4. Add soy sauce, oyster suace, chinese cooking wine, salt, sugark, and dark soy sauce.
    5. Once seasonings are combined taste to get flavor how you like it. It will be a little saltier and that's normal.
  3. Crafting the Stuffed Buns
    1. Take your dough and divide it into equally sized balls. Sprinkle flour and roll each ball into a circle.
      • Make sure the center of the circle is thicker than the edges. It needs to be able to support the filling firmly.
    2. Place a scoop of filling onto the center of the circle.
    3. WIth your thumb keeping the filling in place form pleats along the edges of the bun before twisting the excess into a knot at the top.
      • It's best to watch the videos in the source section to get the technique right.
  4. Steaming the Buns
    1. Prepare your steamer by placing parchment paper cut into squares at the bottom of each section.
    2. Place each bun onto the parchment paper and make sure to leave enough space between each bun for expansion. Spritz with a little water but not too much.
    3. Place steamer in a pot of slightly warm water and let sit for 15 to 30 minutes.
    4. Your Baozi should have increased in sizel. Now increase the heat to high and bring the water to a boil until you see steam.
    5. Turn the heat down to about medium temperature and steam for 15 minutes
    6. Turn off the heat and let the buns sit for 5 minutes before removing the lid to prevent them from collapsing.
    7. Remove the buns, serve up, eat and ENJOY!
